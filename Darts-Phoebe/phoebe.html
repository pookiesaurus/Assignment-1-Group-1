Darts, Where do I start?

Darts, a competitive and casually enjoyed sport / game, is an indoor throwing game where players through small projectiles (Darts) at a board to score points. Points are collated by where specifically players hit – which are distributed individually across the board, often within the goal to deduct a certain number of points within standard play (501).

Starting with darts, the main information to know is the rules, strategy and scoring of the game, then you can move onto playing different game modes & types, be it with friends

Rules of Gameplay

To play darts, some overall guidelines must be followed to ensure a fair, accurate and
safe game is conducted. This includes how players organise the game setup,
organisation of turn order and how each game should be operated.

Play rules:

  • To decide playing order: each player starts the game by throwing a single dart
    each, with the throw l anding closest to the bullseye going first.
  • Each turn, traditionally a player is tasked throw three darts, unless the game
    is won in less.
  • Points may only be calculated for darts that reach the board at the end of a turn,
    any darts that bounce or fall cannot be thrown again.
  • If a player scores more than their remaining points required to win, their throw
    ends and is scored zero.
    (E.g.: A player has 16 remaining and accidentally hit a 20 with their first dart, their
    turn is ended, and they receive no points).
  • Players must throw darts from a specific distance from the board (See setup
    below)
  • Darts games traditionally consist of 'Legs' (Rounds), where each game is resolved
    after a set amount of 'legs' are won.

Setup:

Dartboards should be set up within a safe and comfortable location for players,
optimally within an environment without any potential hazards – Spaces that are well lit,
well mounted and have protection on the floors, walls and other surfaces allow for
gameplay puncture-free.

For official setup rules, the main requirements are as follows:

  • The centre of the board (the Bullseye) should be 173cm high from the ground, or
    137cm for players in wheelchairs.
  • Players must throw from a clearly marked throwline, of a set distance of 237cm
    horizontally from the board, for both standing and seated players.
    (Extra) throwing space is recommended to be at least 152cm W x 3.35m
    in size, to allow for safe distances between players, the board to protect
    players and any items within the game-space.

How Scoring works

Dartboards are divided into distinct sections
– The Bulls (Inner and Outer), Individual
numbers, and doubling or treble sections.

The Bulls

Includes both the main and outer Bullseyes – the circle points at the centre of the board. Hitting this area scores 50 points for the centre Bullseye and 25 for the outer bull ring.


Individual numbers

This includes all the wide spaces on the dartboard that are not within the narrow rings or the bullseye. Hitting one of these sections will award you the number of points as written in the corresponding section at the end of the board. These are 'Single-Scoring', as they only award 1 multiple of the points allocated.

Double / Treble rings

These areas are the two rings seen in the middle and edge of the board. (Usually green & red on the dart board). The outer ring – the Double – when hit grant the player a multiplier of 2 on the points on the outer edge of the board. (Alike individuals, the base point number is calculated from the outer edge numbers)

The inner ring – The Treble – Score the player a multiplier of 3 to the corresponding number on the outer edge board.

Both Treble & Double rings only apply to the throw and dart that hits it. (Multipliers do not count across multiple throws)

Bounced / Fallen darts

Failing to hit the board or darts that do not remain on the board at the end of the round do not score any points. Whoops!

Darts: Other gamemodes

Within the world of darts, there are a wide collection of alternative game modes to 501
that are played which provide new outlooks or methods of darts games for fun changes
and new difficulties.

Gamemode Players Active Numbers Rules
301 Any.
(usually in 2 teams) 		All Numbers. (Alike 501) Each team starts with 301 points, with the goal to reach zero by subtracting points through each throw & turn. Hitting more than your total points will lead to a loss of turn.
Cricket 2 Players.
(usually in 2 teams) 		Bullseye,
20,19,18,17,16,15 		The aim is to 'close' each of the active numbers on the board (By hitting 3 of a number with the dart in a row) This can include individuals, doubles or trebles. Once a number is 'closed' the player that closed it can score points on it until the opponent closes it as well. All numerical scores are added together after all innings are closed, with the player who closes all first and has the most point wins. Hitting more than your total points will lead to a loss of turn.
Killer Any
(3 recommended)		 Determined by players:
Each player throws a dart with their non-dominant hand to randomly choose 'their' number. 		In each round, a player tries to hit their double of their number (with their 3 shots) and Once a player is a killer, a killer aims to hit their opponent’s double number. Taking a life every time, a double is hit (including the killer’s own), each player has 3 lives. Players who failed to become a killer will have an opportunity to become one each turn, until their lives are lost, or they achieve killer status. The last player standing is the winner.
Around the World 2 Players
(or teams) 		All except Bullseye The goal is to be the first player to hit each number 1-20 on the board in order. Any part of the number is included (Individual, Double or trebles). Players take turns after 3 shots, but each throw must be in a consecutive sequence. Players cannot move up otherwise.
