BOWLING

O V E R V I E W

Bowling is a popular recreational activity played by people of all ages.

The term most commonly refers to ten-pin bowling, a competitive game in which players aim to knock down a set of ten pins using a bowling ball. Its history can be traced all the way back to ancient Egypt, where participants rolled stones at different objects with the aim of knocking them over. Nowadays, the most popular form of the sport involves a narrow lane, pins, and a bowling ball of the player's preferred size and weight. Bowling can be played individually or in teams, with leagues and tournaments held worldwide. It requires skill, technique, and strategy, as players must adjust their approach based on lane conditions, ball weight, and pin placement.

S E T - U P

  • Ten pins are set up in a triangular pattern with a base of 6 at the end of the lane
  • The player then selects their chosen bowling ball from the dispenser
  • The game can be played with the gutter guards up or down, depending on the players' preference

R U L E S

  • There are ten frames in the scoreboard, and each player gets to bowl twice in their given frame
  • At the end of their turn, the pins reset and the next player may begin their attempt and scores are recorded after each turn in a frame
  • The number of pins knocked down in each frame translates to the the player's score and the player with the highest score after all ten frames are played is the declared the winner
  • The player with the highest score is declared the winner of the round

T E R M I N O L O G Y

Frame: A frame refers to 1/10 of the bowling game. Each game has ten frames and a player bowls twice in one frame
Strike: All ten pins are knocked down in one turn, or using 1/2 of their given frame
Double: Scoring a strike on each turn of a given frame
Spare: Some pins are knocked down in the first bowl of the frame, while the rest are knocked down in the second bowl, meaning that all ten pins are knocked down after one frame
Split: When the remaining pins after a bowl are positioned on either side of the lane, leaving a gap in the centre
Open Frame: When there are pins left standing after bowling twice in one frame

HOW TO BOWL:

  • Before beginning, it is important to choose the right weight of ball suitable correct size to match your fingers, and a general to rule is that the ball should weigh 10% of your body weight and the holes should fit snuggly around your fingers
  • Hold the ball slightly to your side, with your bowling hand resting underneath and your other hand on the bottom-side for support
  • Approach the foul line with your back straight, shoulders squared, chest facing the target and knees at a slight bend
  • Lower the ball as you approach the foul line, feet slightly apart, ending with one foot slightly behind (this would be the foot opposite to your bowling hand)
  • It may be easiest to aim at the centre of the pin set-up, and once more comfortable, work on aiming for the left or right side of the tip
  • Finally, release the ball while maintaining a straightforward approach. Swing the bowling ball smoothly back and then forward, releasing when your arm has moved as far as you can reach
  • Keep your eye on the target while releasing the ball, with your thumb coming out first and then your fingers
  • Now keep practicing and enjoy!
